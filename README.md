## Answers to Required Questions

Your presentation addresses all 5 questions:

### 1. What do Latin sources tell us?
**Covered in:** Slides 2-9
- 8 Latin quotations with translations
- Shows Roman perspective on wall's purpose, construction, and impact

### 2. How reliable are these sources?
**Covered in:** Slides 2-9 (within each source analysis) + Slide 14
- Discusses bias (word choice like "barbaros")
- Notes date gaps (Historia Augusta written 180 years later)
- Identifies propaganda vs. fact
- Points out missing British voices

### 3. What does archaeology confirm or contradict?
**Covered in:** Slides 11-14
- Construction phases show plan changes (Broad Wall vs. Narrow Wall)
- Gates every mile contradict "absolute separation" narrative
- Physical remains confirm scale and legionary construction
- Slide 14 has detailed comparison grid

### 4. How did occupation affect Romans and Britons?
**Covered in:** Slide 15
- Impact on Romans: resource drain, isolation, policy shift
- Impact on Britons: land seizure, displacement, cultural suppression, but also trade opportunities
- Discusses complex colonial relationship

### 5. What long-term legacy did this leave?
**Covered in:** Slide 16
- Geographic divide (Scotland-England)
- Infrastructure (roads, towns)
- Language (place names with Latin roots)
- Cultural memory and tourism
- Modern identity and popular culture

## Presentation Tips

### For Delivering the Presentation:

1. **Start with Impact:** Slide 1 sets the scene. Emphasize the scale: 73 miles, 6-8 years to build, 15,000 soldiers.

2. **Focus on Bias:** When showing Latin sources (Slides 2-9), really dig into word choices:
   - Why "barbaros" instead of neutral term?
   - Who's missing from these accounts? (British voices!)

3. **Use the Animation:** Slide 11 is your visual centerpiece. Click through all four phases to show how complex the construction was.

4. **Archaeological Evidence:** Slide 14's comparison grid is crucial‚Äîshows you're thinking critically, not just accepting Roman propaganda.

5. **Both Perspectives:** Slide 15 shows you understand both sides‚Äînot just "Rome bad, Britons good" but complex colonial relationship.

6. **Modern Connection:** Slide 16 brings it home‚Äîwhy this 1900-year-old wall still matters today.

### Timing Guide (for a 20-minute presentation):

- Slides 1-2: 2 minutes (intro + first source)
- Slides 3-9: 8 minutes (~1 min per source, focus extra on Sources 1, 6, 7, 8)
- Slides 10-11: 3 minutes (timeline + animation demo)
- Slides 12-13: 2 minutes (map + photos)
- Slides 14-16: 4 minutes (analysis + impact + legacy)
- Slide 17: 1 minute (conclusion)

### If You Have More/Less Time:

**30 minutes:** Spend more time on each Latin source, read them aloud in Latin, discuss each guiding question in depth.

**15 minutes:** Skip to key slides: 1, 2 (source 1), 4 (source 3), 7 (source 6), 11 (animation), 14 (archaeology), 15 (impact), 17 (conclusion).
